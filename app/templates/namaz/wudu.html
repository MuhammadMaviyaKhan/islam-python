Wudu (Ablution) - Shia Method

Purpose

Wudu is a ritual purification required before Salah (prayer) and touching the text of the Quran. It is based on Quran 5:6: "O you who have believed, when you rise to [perform] prayer, wash your faces and your forearms to the elbows and wipe over your heads and wash your feet to the ankles."

Step-by-Step Guide (Shia Fiqh)
  1. Niyyah (Intention): Make intention that you are performing Wudu for the pleasure of Allah and to become purified for prayer.
  2. Wash Face: Wash your face from the forehead to the chin (not including the neck). Water must flow on the skin, not just wiped.
  3. Wash Right Arm: Wash your right arm from the elbow to the fingertips. Water must flow from the elbow downward.
  4. Wash Left Arm: Repeat for the left arm.
  5. Wipe Head (Masah): With the moisture remaining on your right hand, wipe the front part of your head (the area from forehead to crown of head). Do not take fresh water.
  6. Wipe Right Foot: With the remaining moisture, wipe the top of your right foot from the toes to the ankle.
  7. Wipe Left Foot: Repeat for the left foot.
Important: In Shia fiqh, the feet are wiped (masah), not washed. Washing the feet invalidates the wudu according to Shia jurisprudence.
Common Mistakes
  • Using more water than necessary (waste is forbidden)
  • Not ensuring water flows on the skin (hair or barriers prevent wudu)
  • Washing the feet instead of wiping
  • Wiping the neck or back of the head (not part of Shia wudu)
Things That Invalidate Wudu
  • Natural discharge (urine, stool, gas)
  • Sleep (when it overcomes the senses)
  • Anything that causes loss of consciousness
  • Menses and postnatal bleeding for women
